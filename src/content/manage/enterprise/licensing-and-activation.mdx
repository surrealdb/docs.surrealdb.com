# Licensing & activation

Your **licence key** encodes product edition, seat or capacity limits, and the subscription term. Store it in a secrets manager and inject it at runtime; rotate keys when your contract renews or when your security policy requires it. If you run several environments (development, staging, production), use separate keys or clearly separated secret namespaces so a mistake in one cluster cannot invalidate another.

**Activation** typically happens when the EE binary starts and can reach SurrealDB’s licensing endpoints. The server exchanges a minimal fingerprint with the service to validate the key and record entitlement—without sending your application data.

If your environment blocks outbound access, use **offline activation**: generate a request payload from the server, submit it through your approved channel, import the signed response, and restart or reload as documented for your release. Offline bundles are common for air-gapped data centres and regulated networks.

**Renewal** extends the validity window on the vendor side; you may receive a replacement key or an update file before expiry. Plan renewals ahead of time to avoid service interruption when the key reaches end-of-term.

For day-to-day operations, monitor logs for licence warnings and align your change-management process with [Installation](/docs/manage/enterprise) so upgrades and failovers preserve a valid activated state.
