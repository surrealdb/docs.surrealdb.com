# Support tiers

Enterprise Edition customers choose a **support tier** that sets coverage hours, response expectations, and escalation paths. Names and entitlements are confirmed in your order; typical tiers are **Standard**, **Premium**, and **Mission-Critical**.

| Tier | What it usually includes | Escalation path |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Standard** | Business-hours access, portal or email cases, documented response targets, knowledge base and release notes | Through the standard support queue to engineering when a defect is confirmed |
| **Premium** | Faster responses for high-impact issues, broader coverage windows, stronger coordination for production incidents | Priority queue; senior support engineers as per contract |
| **Mission-Critical** | Shortest initial response for sev-1 events; optional named contacts or phone bridges | Expedited escalation to specialised engineers and management visibility per agreement |

**Escalation** should follow your runbook: front-line triage, then deeper engineering if logs and reproductions show a product defect. For contractual response and uptime commitments, align your internal severity definitions with [SLAs](/docs/manage/enterprise/support/slas) and your signed agreement.
