# FIPS

Some organisations require **FIPS 140-2** validated cryptography for data in transit and at rest. SurrealDB Enterprise Edition can be built and shipped with **FIPS-validated cryptographic modules** where your platform vendor provides a supported provider (for example OpenSSL FIPS or the equivalent your distribution documents).

**Which modules apply** depends on the EE build you run: the release notes and security data sheet name the validated provider, version, and certificate identifiers. Use only the combinations your compliance team has approved.

To **enable FIPS mode**, follow the configuration flags or environment variables documented for your EE version—typically forcing the TLS stack and internal crypto primitives to call into the FIPS provider exclusively. Avoid mixing non-validated algorithms when FIPS is required.

Validate the deployment with your standard crypto checks: confirm TLS handshakes use approved cipher suites, run vulnerability scans appropriate to your sector, and keep the underlying modules patched within your vendor’s guidance.
