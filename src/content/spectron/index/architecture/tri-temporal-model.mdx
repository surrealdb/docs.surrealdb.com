# Tri-temporal model

Spectron does not treat “delete row” as the primary way to retire a belief. Memories are **superseded** or **aged**; three clocks answer three different questions.

## The three clocks

| Clock | What it is | Answers |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **System time** | SurrealDB **MVCC** row history – every write is a new row version; `SELECT … VERSION d'…'` can reconstruct substrate state at an instant. | “What did the **bytes in the database** look like at instant *T*?” |
| **Known time** | The **supersession-chain** clock: when Spectron first asserted a belief; `as_of` queries walk the chain of beliefs. | “When did **we** first believe this?” |
| **Valid time** | Real-world validity: `valid_from` / `valid_until` describe when the assertion held **in the world**, independent of when Spectron learnt it. | “When was this **true**?” |

Document-authored facts may carry a `valid_from` years before ingestion; conversational facts typically anchor to turn time unless you supply otherwise.

## What you get in practice

- **`VERSION` queries** – substrate-level rewind without application joins.
- **`as_of` queries** – belief-level rewind over supersession chains, indexed for retrieval.
- **Supersession chains** – prior beliefs remain addressable with `valid_until` set; history of how a fact changed stays queryable.
- **Aging, not deletion** – observations untouched over a window can be marked superseded with a reason in `source.history` rather than erased. Trace-derived reinforcement can outlive a trace row that has aged out.
- **`forget` as a first-class verb** – explicit user-driven removal exists and is distinct from aging. The CLI exposes both.

Together with [provenance](/docs/spectron/mental-model/provenance-and-traceability) and a **single reconciler** ([Eight pillars](/docs/spectron/architecture/eight-pillars-and-categories)), this yields a **defensible audit story**: unwind any current fact to what it replaced, to the source that produced it, and to the exact bytes that source referenced – and rewind the whole substrate by system time while still querying belief history on known time.

For temporal fields in the reasoning model docs, see [Temporal validity](/docs/spectron/memory/reasoning-model/temporal-validity) and [Reconciliation and supersession](/docs/spectron/memory/reasoning-model/reconciliation-and-supersession).
